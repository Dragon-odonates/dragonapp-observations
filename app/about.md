## Objectives

The aim of this dashboard is to visually explore the odonates observations gather by the [**Dragon** consortium](https://www.fondationbiodiversite.fr/en/the-frb-in-action/programs-and-projects/le-cesab/dragon/).   

You can select a species, and visualize the spatial distribution on the period 2000-2024 (`average`), its temporal trend (`slope`) or the dynamic of its annual observations (`dynamic`).  

The values shown are not the average probabilities, but the square root transformation of the ratio between the number of reported occurrences, and the number of observation events.   

Be aware of the multiple biases due to non structured data collection from citizen science programs; the dynamics observed may not be the true dynamic and is influenced by the observers dynamics.    

This is a beta version. Data curation is still in progress, if you have any question or spot any mistake, please contact Lisa.  


## About Dragon

The [**Dragon** project](https://www.fondationbiodiversite.fr/en/the-frb-in-action/programs-and-projects/le-cesab/dragon/) focuses on odonates (dragonflies and damselflies), a charismatic and important but often neglected taxonomic group that provides a unique opportunity to **study anthropogenic impacts on interface ecosystems linking terrestrial and freshwater environments**. The characteristics of this group make it a good candidate for the development of a new indicators to inform and support environmental management.

The research project **Dragon** was selected from the [2022 FRB-MTE-OFB "Antropogenic pressures and impacts on terrestrial biodiversity](https://www.fondationbiodiversite.fr/en/calls/appel-a-projets-frb-mte-ofb-2022-impacts-sur-la-biodiversite-terrestre-dans-lanthropocene/).  


Source code is available on [Github](https://github.com/Dragon-odonates/dragonapp-observations).
